Exercise 4 - Terminal basic exercise part 2

1. What does the man command do? Type in man rm. How do you scroll and get out?
   • The man command is used to show what a particular command does. When you type in man rm it shows you what the rm command does and you can the arrow keys to scroll up and down and press q to get out.

2. Look at the man page for ls. What does the -l flag do? What does the -a flag do?
   • The -l flag gives information about the files while the -a flag prints all the files including entries starting with a ‘.’.

3. Type the following command to download and save the contents of google.com: curl https://www.google.com > google.html

4. Use less to look at the contents of google.html.

5. Look at the man page for less. Read the section on /pattern. Search for the text hplogo in the google.html file.

6. How do you jump between words in the terminal?
   • Alt + forward or backward arrow.

7. How do you get to the end of a line in terminal?
   • Ctrl + E

8. How do you move your cursor to the beginning in terminal?
   • Ctrl + A

9. How do you delete a word (without pressing backspace multiple times) in terminal?
   • Alt + backspace, Ctrl + W

10. What is the difference between a terminal and shell?
    • A shell is a program that actually runs the command while a terminal is a wrapper program that runs a shell.

11. What is an absolute path?
    • An absolute part give a specific direction to a file or folder regardless of your current location in the terminal.

12. What is a relative path?
    • A relative path specifies a direction in relation to your current location on the terminal.

13. What is a flag? Give three examples of flags you have used.
    • Flags are commands that are used in addition to main commands to provide extra functionality.

14. What do the r and f flags do with the rm command?
    • The r flag goes through files and folders recursively while the f flag forces removal.
